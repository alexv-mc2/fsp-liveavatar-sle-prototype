## D4. Classification criteria versus clinical diagnosis

- 2019 EULAR/ACR classification requires ANA positivity at least once as an entry criterion, at least one clinical item, attribution only when no more likely explanation exists, and a total score of at least 10. Only the highest-weight item in each domain is counted. [S3][S4]
- These criteria were designed primarily to define research populations. They can structure diagnostic thinking but do not replace an individualized physician diagnosis. [S3][S4][S6][S7]
- A classification score must never be shown to the patient as proof that the diagnosis is certain. [INFERENCE from S3][S4][S6][S7]

## D7. Pregnancy, contraception and antiphospholipid considerations

- Pregnancy is often possible but should be planned in an inactive disease phase and managed by experienced rheumatology and obstetric teams. Active SLE, active lupus nephritis, APS and aPL positivity increase risk. [S3]
- Contraception choice should reflect disease activity and thromboembolic/aPL risk. IUDs can generally be offered absent gynecologic contraindications; combined oral contraception may be considered only in stable/inactive SLE without aPL. [S3]
- APS is not diagnosed from one antibody result. High-risk aPL profiles rely on persistent findings on repeat testing at least 12 weeks apart, and clinical thrombotic or obstetric history matters. [S3][S7]
- In this prototype, copper IUD use, no thrombosis/miscarriage, and one negative aPL panel keep the topic relevant but prevent it from dominating the case. [PROTOTYPE][REVIEW]

Labor: leichte normozytäre Anämie, Leukopenie, BSG↑ bei CRP im Referenzbereich, ANA 1:640, Anti-dsDNA und Anti-Sm positiv, C3/C4 erniedrigt; Kreatinin/eGFR, Urinstatus, Sediment und UPCR unauffällig. Dringender V. a. Erstmanifestation eines SLE mit kutaner, muskuloskelettaler und hämatologischer Beteiligung, derzeit kein Hinweis auf Lupusnephritis oder akute Organbedrohung. Zeitnahe rheumatologische Diagnoseverifizierung und differenzialdiagnostische Abklärung; ambulantes Vorgehen bei gesicherter rascher Nachsorge.“

## H3. Documentation quality checklist

- Use chronology and objective values; do not write „Fieber“ when only 38.2 °C was reported.
- Distinguish „Arthralgie“ from documented „Arthritis/Gelenkbeteiligung“.
- Write „V. a.“ or „dringender Verdacht“, not „gesicherter SLE“.
- Record relevant negatives without a full review-of-systems dump.
- Do not omit medication dose/frequency, allergy reaction, gynecologic history, thrombosis/miscarriage history, renal screen or disposition rationale.
- Use medical German in the form; patient wording belongs in the interview, not the final handover.

## I2. Full model handover in German

Thorakale, pulmonale, neurologische und renale Warnsymptome werden verneint; ebenso Thrombosen und Fehlgeburten. Sie nimmt seit zwei Wochen Ibuprofen 400 mg bei Bedarf, etwa drei- bis viermal pro Woche.

## K5. Common learner mistakes

| Mistake | Why it matters | Correction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Using Fachsprache with the patient | Reduces comprehensibility in the APG. | Explain first in lay language; use the term only after explanation. [S1] |
| Declaring SLE from ANA | Medically overconfident and linguistically unsafe. | Use „Verdacht“ and explain that ANA is supportive, not definitive. [S3][S4][S6][S7] |
| Calling 38.2 °C fever criterion positive | Incorrect classification use. | Document „subfebril“ and award 0 criterion points. [S4][S6] |
| Adding anti-Sm and anti-dsDNA points | Violates same-domain rule. | Maximum 6 points in that domain. [S3][S4] |
| Not asking renal questions or urine tests | May miss clinically silent nephritis. | Ask urine/edema questions and name urinalysis, sediment and UPCR. [S3][S5][S6] |
| Ignoring pregnancy / thrombosis history | Misses a relevant SLE/APS safety domain. | Ask LMP, contraception, pregnancy plans, miscarriage and thrombosis. [S3] |
| Calling anemia hemolytic without evidence | Over-interpretation. | State normocytic anemia and request hemolysis/other-cause work-up. [S4][S6] |
| Treating normal CRP as infection exclusion | Unsafe. | Interpret in context and continue infection assessment. [S4][S6] |
| Blanket NSAID advice | Medication risk not individualized. | Check contraindications and avoid prescribing from the simulation. [REVIEW] |
| Overloading the handover | Obscures the clinical pattern and tests fluency poorly. | Synthesize by timeline, organ domains, assessment and plan. |
| Mixing patient and doctor register | FSP tests register switching. | Use lay language in station 1 and Fachsprache in station 3. [S1] |
| Inventing facts when unsure | Breaks case integrity and can create unsafe feedback. | Use „nicht erhoben“, „unklar“ or ask for the missing information. |

## L4. Scoring checklist

- Record evidence as exact learner utterances or documentation snippets; avoid impression-only ratings.
- Use „not observed“ rather than zero when the scenario or examiner did not create the opportunity.
- Do not reward memorized specialist detail that makes the patient interaction less understandable.
- Penalize fabricated facts and internal contradictions in both language and safety layers.
- Give corrective examples in the same register: patient-friendly correction for APG; Fachsprache correction for handover/documentation.

# M. Guardrails and safety policy

| Guardrail | Required behavior |
| --- | --- |
| Fictional case boundary | Always identify Frau Hartmann as fictional training content. Never imply that the simulation is medical care. |
| No real-user diagnosis | If the learner asks about their own symptoms, stop the patient role and state that the simulation cannot assess or diagnose them. Recommend evaluation by a real clinician; for emergency symptoms, direct to emergency services. |
| No treatment outside the case | Do not tell a real user to start, stop or change medication. In the fictional case, keep treatment at high-level educational framing unless reviewed content explicitly permits more. |
| Diagnostic uncertainty | Use „Verdacht“, „vereinbar mit“ and „muss abgeklärt werden“. Never treat classification as diagnostic proof. [S3][S4][S6][S7] |
| Medication safety | No unreviewed dosing, contraindication-free NSAID reassurance, steroid initiation, immunosuppressant selection or pregnancy-medication advice. |
| Real patient data | If a user enters name, date of birth, address, insurance number, exact dates or other identifiers from a real patient, ask them to remove/anonymize the data before continuing. |
| Emergency escalation | If a real user reports chest pain, severe dyspnea, hemoptysis, seizure, focal deficit, marked confusion, severe bleeding, oliguria/AKI signs, thrombosis symptoms, or serious deterioration, exit role-play and recommend urgent emergency evaluation. |
| Case-state integrity | The avatar may only use the canonical fact matrix. Unknown facts remain unknown; no improvisation of labs, pregnancy, medication, family history or red flags. |
| Patient/examiner separation | The patient must not access examiner-only facts, scoring rules, classification points or model answers. |
| Language versus clinical feedback | Return two distinct analyses: (1) German/FSP communication and (2) medical correctness. Add a separate safety gate. |
| Source traceability | Every medical correction carries source IDs; prototype choices carry [PROTOTYPE]; inferred triage decisions carry [INFERENCE][REVIEW]. |
| Human review | Any content involving disposition, rash attribution, medication, pregnancy, APS, laboratory reference ranges or emergency thresholds requires physician approval before deployment. |
| No official claim | Never state that the case, scripts, rubric or question bank are issued, approved or endorsed by Ärztekammer Nordrhein. |
